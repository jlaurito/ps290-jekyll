## School Lunch and Breakfast Menu

School meals are **free** for all students who would like one. Families do not need to sign up students to receive school lunch.

See the Department of Education ["Pre-K to 8th grade" daily lunch menu](https://www.schools.nyc.gov/school-life/food/menus/school-lunch-meals?value=s8#menuDiv).

Each day there is a daily lunch special (usually served hot), such as turkey burger, pizza, tacos, or chicken. There is also a basic offering, featured in the "Daily Offerings" (served cold), such as a sandwich and sides. Meal deliveries depend on availability and are subject to change from what is posted in the online menu.

We also serve free breakfast to enrolled students every day. Cold, pre-packed breakfasts are served during early arrival (**8:00–8:15am**) for students in kindergarten through 5th grade. Students in 3-K and Pre-K are given breakfast in their classrooms after morning arrival. Breakfast items are also offered to any student who would like them at the beginning of the school day. See the [breakfast menu](https://www.schools.nyc.gov/school-life/food/menus/breakfast-meals?value=s4#menuDiv).

Students may also bring a nut-free lunch from home, and many do. Check with your child's teacher to see if there are any students with food allergies in the class in case certain foods should be avoided.

Additional information, including nutritional content and allergy guidance, can be found on the [DOE menu & nutrition information page](https://www.schools.nyc.gov/school-life/food/menu-nutrition-information).

### School Lunch Form: Required of Each Student

Be sure to complete your [School Lunch Form](https://www.myschoolapps.com/Home/PickDistrict) each year, regardless of whether your child has school lunch or brings their own. This impacts school funding. You're not required to provide any personal information.

### Free Meals for Students

All enrolled students can receive free breakfast/lunch in school.

**Free Food Locations.** NYC has a map of locations that offer free food (food pantries, soup kitchens), as well as farmers' markets and grocery stores. Please visit a [map of food resources across the city](https://finder.nyc.gov/foodhelp/).

## Annual Appeal

Each year the PTA raises money to cover school expenditures and programs not paid for by the Department of Education. Our major fundraiser each year is the Annual Appeal. This money helps pay for our school's exceptional teaching assistant staff, music and arts programs, science supplies, and much more.

**Donations can be made at: [mnspta.org/donate](https://mnspta.org/donate).**
